A short review on the topological materials and the topological order

Posted on 2021-11-30 In Reviews , Short Reviews Disqus:

From Hall Effect to topological materials

The entire field can be traced back to the earliest when the Hall effect was discovered. In 1879, Edwin Hall, a Ph.D. student at Johns Hopkins University in Maryland, discovered the Hall effect (QE). [1] This phenomenon indicates that a voltage proportional to the magnetic field will be generated in the direction perpendicular to the current and the applied magnetic field.

With the advancement of technology, the German physicist von Klitzing at Max Planck Institute discovered that when the magnetic field increases to a certain extent, the Hall voltage actually rises in steps. This is called the Integer Quantum Hall Effect (IQHE). [2] The integers appearing in the Hall effect are some topological quantum numbers, called Chern numbers. After observing a non-integer Hall voltage. The Fractional Quantum Hall Effect (FQHE) was discovered by Robert Laughlin, Daniel Tsui and others. [3][4]

In 2005, a new study guided the Quantum Spin Hall Effect (QSHE), which is essentially the quantization of the abnormal Hall effect part. [5] It brings us a new state of matter. We call them two-dimensional topological insulators (TI), and they have special edge effects: there are some special quantum states on the surface.

If one material is topologically nontrivial and another material is topologically trivial, put them together. There will be a topological phase change on the contact surface, and this phase change will have some physical phenomena: for example, the surface conductivity of topological insulators. In order to describe this phase transition, we need to find the topological invariants, such as Chern number [6] and Z2 invariant [7], or hopf fibration [8], topological order [9] and returning Thouless pump [8]. Based on Pontrjagin's research on the dual-band Hamiltonian in the three-momentum dimension, Dr. Alexandradinata's team studied a new topological invariant: returning Thouless pump (RTP), and did a detailed study of the Hopf-RTP relation. Generally speaking, different topological quantum states can be described by different topological invariants. Therefore, determining the topological invariant of the electronic structure of a material system is the core of judging its topological properties. [10][11] This topological invariant is closely related to Berry’s phase [12][13], so Chern–Gauss–Bonnet theorem can be a very helpful tool. [14] Dr. Alexandradinata and Dr. Syzranov of UCSC both have important contribution in this field. [15] [8] [16] Some other information that I would like to check during phd are [17][18][19]. Dr. Alexandradinata most recent reseearch[8]

Quantum Spin Liquid and topological order

"Quantum Spin Liquid" (QSL) was proposed by Anderson in a 1973 paper. In the original "Resonating Valence Bond" theory (RVB), there are some errors, so it has not received the attention it deserves. [20] After a long time, due to the discovery of high-temperature superconductivity, people renewed emphasis on quantum spin liquid, and Resonating Valence Bond and Valence Bond State (VBS) were gradually improved. [21] Spin liquid refers to spin frustration caused by a certain geometric symmetry or the complexity of interaction, a state in which a ground state presents a disordered state, including spin glass and quantum spin liquid. The biggest difference between quantum spin liquid and the former is the existence of long-range RVB. This quantum fluctuation means the existence of long-range entanglement, which is of great help to our understanding of entanglement. [24] QSL state has nothing to do with spontaneous symmetry breaking [22], so it does not involve Landau phase transition theory [23]. Our understanding of it may require a deeper understanding of topological order. This makes QSL research has great potential. The existence of long-range entanglement and strong coherence makes this field possible to lead topological quantum computing and quantum communication to make more progress in the future. This review is of great help to me in understanding the recent developments in the field of QSL research. [24] Dr. Syzranov's paper is also helpful here. [25]

In the previous section we mentioned the idea of topological order. In 1989, Wen Xiaogang put the fractional quantum Hall state on different topological manifolds to probe the new paradigm implicit in the fractional quantum Hall state. This brings us to the concept of topological order. [26][9] Different from Landau's symmetry breaking theory, topological order is a new state of matter. I don't know enough about it. I will carefully drill the basic theory in the phd stage to prepare for research. Citing the original paper here. [27]
