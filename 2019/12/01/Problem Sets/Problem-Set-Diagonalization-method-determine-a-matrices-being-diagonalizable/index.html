Question

!!! info “Example Question:” Use the Diagonalization Method to determine whether each of the following matrices is diagonalizable. If so, specify the matrices $\mathbf{D}$ and $\mathbf{P}$ and check your work by verifying that $\mathbf{D}=\mathbf{P}^{-1} \mathbf{A} \mathbf{P}$. $$\star(g) \qquad \mathbf{A}=\begin{equation} \left[\begin{array}{rrr} 2 & 0 & 0 \ -3 & 4 & 1 \ 3 & -2 & 1 \end{array}\right] \end{equation}$$

Solution

First of all, take the characteristic polynomial of $\mathbf{A}$ and solve for eigenvalues.

So, we have that the eigenvalues of $\mathbf{A}$ are $x_1=2$ and $x_2=3$. For eigenvalue $x_1=2$, have linear system:

$$ \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}{2-2} & {0} & {0} & {0} \ {3} & {2-4} & {-1} & {0} \ {-3} & {2} & {2-1} & {0}\end{array}\right] \Longrightarrow \left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}{1} & {-\frac{2}{3}} & {-\frac{1}{3}} & {0} \ {0} & {0} & {0} & {0} \ {0} & {0} & {0} & {0}\end{array}\right] $$

So, we have $2$ independent eigenvectors $[1, 0, 3]$ and $[2, 3, 0]$.
