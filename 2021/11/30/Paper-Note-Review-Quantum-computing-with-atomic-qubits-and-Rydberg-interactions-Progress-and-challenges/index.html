Notes and Future visions

It is tempting to focus on gate fidelity as the most important challenge facing neutral atom quantum computation.

Optical traps for neutral atoms cannot be arbitrarily deep.

To minimize heating and decoherence rates the traps should be designed to satisfy a ground-Rydberg magic condition.

  • What is the probability of having 1 bit lost in ERROR CORRECTION?
    • \(\boxed{P_{\text {loss }}=N_{\text {code }}\left(1-e^{-t / \tau_{\text {vac }}}\right) \simeq N_{\text {code }} t / \tau_{\text {vac }}}\)
      • \(N_{\text {code }}\): # of qubits of quantum error correcting code
      • \(\tau_{\mathrm{vac}}\): vacuum limited lifetime of each qubit
      • \(P_{\text {loss }}\): probability of having lost at least one bit after time \(t\)
    • in multiple qubits, have \(\boxed{\mathcal{O}(1 / N)}\) as efficiency of loss
  • If error being correctable, how much time we need for this correction?
    • \(\boxed{\tau_{\mathrm{vac}}>\frac{N_{\mathrm{code}} t_{\mathrm{qec}}}{\epsilon}}\)
      • \(t_{\mathrm{qec}}\): duration we require for experiments, \(t_{\mathrm{qec}}=0.1 N_{\mathrm{code}}\)
  • If error being correctable, how much time we need for reloading?
