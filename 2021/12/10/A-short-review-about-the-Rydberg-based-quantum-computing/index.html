Physics Background

General Background on Quantum Computing

In the last century, after the Turing machine model made a major breakthrough in classical computers, scientists have been considering the possibilities of Hyper computation models. One of those models is the quantum computing. The concept of quantum computing was first proposed by the famous physicist Feynman in 1981. [1] This is a design that uses unitary operator and Hilbert space as the description of the system to express effectively computable information. Specifically, each qubit is in superposition of $\left|0\right\rangle$ and $\left|1\right\rangle$ eigenstates, and the entanglement and quantum decoherence effects enable us to get a quantum superposition state in the system $\sum_{i=0}^{2^{n}- 1} a_{i}\left|x_{i}\right\rangle, \left|a_{i}\right|^{2}=1, a_{i}\in\mathbb{C}$, the information that the system can represent grows by $2^n$, while the classical computer grows by $2n$. Therefore, quantum computers are be superior to classical computers in terms of computational complexity. However, both are implementations of the Turing machine computing model, and the computing power is essentially equivalent, and both are effectively computable, as described by Church-Turing Thesis. It is worth mentioning that the computing power of a quantum computer has an upper limit. It is called Bremermann’s limit and the value is $\frac{c^{2}}{\hbar}bits/s$, which is caused by the mechanism of quantum mechanics. Yes, more powerful calculations require more energy so that the computing system will collapse into a black hole.\

In the beginning, Feynman proposed the concept of quantum computing to use simple quantum systems to simulate the evolution of complex and difficult quantum systems, which is efficiency impossible to achieve on classical computers. The idea he put forward at the time seems to be more like a quantum simulation than quantum computation. [2][3] In order to show exactly what a quantum computer is, six outstanding quantum physicists proposed that “a quantum computer is a machine that would exploit the full complexity, a many-particle quantum wavefunction to solve a computational problem.” [4] However, a more rigorous statement was put forward by Dr. DiVincenzo. [6] DiVincenzo’s criteria specify the following five necessary conditions for quantum computers:

  • have a sequence of qubits with scalability;
  • qubits and can be initialized;
  • have a long decoherence time;
  • have a sequence of quantum logic gates;
  • the qubits can be measured with some methods.

There are two main aspects to the development of quantum computing, and the separately introduction are as follows.

Firstly is the software level. The initial work focused on the quantum Fourier transform algorithm, which is actually an extension of quantum simulation. But soon, people got a breakthrough in Shor’s algorithm, which can accomplish the 1000-digit factorization in a fraction of a second. [7] This is also the reason why the idea of a general quantum computer being inspired at the first place. Shor’s pioneering work strongly stimulated the development of quantum computers and quantum cryptography. [8] In 1980, Dr. Benioff proved that any computation corresponds to the evolution of a quantum system. [9] This provides the theoretical basis. Other developments to be remembered also include the Quantum Annealing, which reveal the power of quantum computer algorithm in the field of optimization. [10]

More exciting developments are happening at the hardware level. The main quantum computer implementation methods include optical quantum computer [11][12], topological quantum computer [13], quantum dot system, ion trap system [14][15][16], neutral atom quantum computer and superconductor quantum computer. [17][18] The most outstanding results are superconducting quantum computers and ion trap quantum computers. Superconductivity is stronger than ion traps in terms of scalability in the number of qubits, but ion traps are superior in control accuracy and coherence maintenance. As for the others, theoretically, topological quantum computing has the ability to completely isolate external interference, but it has no reliable experimental results so far, so there is not much discussion. The disadvantage of the quantum dot system is that the fidelity of the dual-qubit gate is hovering around 99.0%, plus the lack of scalability. Although it performs great in specific quantum algorithms [19], the optical quantum computer lacks in generalization.\

In the next subsection, I will briefly review the past research on the path of neutral atom systems. Before 2016, for the realization of quantum computing, the neutral atom system did not have obvious competitiveness compared with other systems. The mainstream thinking at that time was still the superconducting loop and ion trap. In fact, until today, these two are still the leading ones on the road of quantum computing: a sequence of computation evaluation parameters including double-qubit gate fidelity [20][21], time-costing [22][23] and quantum decoherence time [24] have performed very well. The scalability of the qubit is also considerable. The universality of the optical quantum computing system is being questioned. The topological quantum computing system has never demonstrated the manipulation of single qubit experimentally. The fidelity of the quantum dot system is poor.

Recent developments of Rydberg atom Quantum Computing

As mentioned in the previous section, the quantum computer of the neutral particle system does not have any outstanding performance. At that time, only the ability of single-atom observation was developed, but the ability of single-atom addressing was not yet available. The rest of the work mainly implemented some simple quantum logic gates. In 2016, the Rydberg neutral particle system ushered in a breakthrough, several different laboratories have completed the preparation of defect-free Rydberg atomic arrays: Dr Jaewook Ahn from KAIS used LCOS-SLM [25], Dr. A. Browaeys from CNRS used SLM and acoustic-optical deflector [26][27] and Dr. MD Lukin from Harvard and Caltech’s Dr. Endres used CCD and acoustic-optical deflector [28]. Dr. A. Browaeys and Dr. M.D. Lukin also implemented a quantum simulator in 2020. [29][30] Using Rydberg neutral particles as a qubit has a series of huge advantages: long coherence time means more stable operation, rich forms of long-range interaction means simpler production of quantum logic gate, The identity of neutral particles means easier entanglement, and experiments show that the Rydberg system has amazing scalability. Despite many many advantages, the Rydberg system still has many problems to be solved. The biggest problem is that the fidelity of the quantum logic gate is still not high enough, and the fidelity drops very seriously when there are a large number of qubits. Moreover, Rydberg laser technology can be improved, and the main work is to reduce line width and various noises. Another point must be mentioned, single qubit addressing in a large-scale atom array is a problem to be solved. From 2020 to 2021, in order to continue to improve the capabilities of the Rydberg quantum computer, physicists have devoted themselves to the study of the many-body interaction of Rydberg atoms. [31][32] I also hope to do this work during my phd career.
